# AI Business Automation Playbook
### 40 Prompts for Automating Your Business Operations with Claude

---

## How to Use
These prompts replace hours of manual business work per week. Copy, customize [BRACKETS], and run. Built for solopreneurs and small teams who need to do everything without a full staff.

---

## SECTION 1: Content & Marketing (8 Prompts)

---

**MKT-01: Content Calendar Creator**

```
Create a 4-week content calendar for [BUSINESS/BRAND NAME] in the [INDUSTRY] space.

Target audience: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMER]
Primary goal: [AWARENESS / LEADS / SALES / RETENTION]
Platforms: [LIST PLATFORMS, e.g., LinkedIn, Instagram, Email, Blog]

For each Monday through Friday entry include:
- Platform
- Topic/Theme
- Hook (opening line or subject line)
- Core message (1 sentence)
- CTA (what you want them to do)

Organize by week. Flag any dates that align with industry events, holidays, or product milestones I should know about.
```

---

**MKT-02: Blog Post Writer**

```
Write a 1,000-word SEO-optimized blog post for [BUSINESS NAME] on the topic: "[BLOG POST TOPIC]"

Target keyword: [PRIMARY KEYWORD]
Secondary keywords: [2-3 SECONDARY KEYWORDS]
Audience: [DESCRIBE READER — experience level, pain points, goals]
Tone: [PROFESSIONAL / CONVERSATIONAL / AUTHORITATIVE]

Format requirements:
- H1 title (include target keyword)
- Meta description (155 characters max)
- 3-4 H2 subheadings
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- At least one bulleted or numbered list
- CTA in final paragraph

Do not use filler phrases like "In today's digital landscape" or "In conclusion."
```

```

**SALES-02: Follow-Up Email Sequence**

```
Write a 5-email follow-up sequence for leads who showed interest in [PRODUCT/SERVICE] but didn't purchase.

- Email 3 (Day 4): Case study or story — real result (or hypothetical example) of transformation your product delivers

- Email 5 (Day 8): Last chance — urgency, recap of benefits, final CTA

For each email include: Subject line, preview text, body copy, CTA.

**SALES-03: Objection Handler**

```
I sell [PRODUCT/SERVICE] at $[PRICE] to [TARGET AUDIENCE].

- Acknowledge and validate the concern (don't dismiss it)
- Reframe the objection with a new perspective
- Provide evidence or a concrete example
- Redirect toward the buying decision

Objections to handle:
1. "It's too expensive."
2. "I don't have time to implement this."
3. "I've tried things like this before and they didn't work."
4. "[CUSTOM OBJECTION SPECIFIC TO YOUR BUSINESS]"
5. "I need to think about it."

Format each as a script I can use in sales calls, DMs, or emails. Keep each response under 100 words.
```

**SALES-04: Discovery Call Script**

```

2. Discovery questions (7-10 questions that uncover pain, timeline, budget, and decision authority)

7. Next steps (what happens after the call)

**CS-03: Refund Response**

```
Write a refund response email for [BUSINESS NAME] to send to [CUSTOMER NAME] who requested a refund for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].
